Projects

Key R&D initiatives combining regulatory expertise, scientific research, and product development

ML Image Analysis Tool for Microbial Monitoring

INRA/CNRS · 2021–2025

Developed an innovative machine learning-based image analysis tool for automated monitoring of microbial interactions and behavioral tracking of microorganisms relevant for crop protection solutions. This project combined computational image analysis with biological validation to create a quantitative framework for understanding microbial dynamics.

Key Achievements

  • Designed and implemented ML algorithms for high-content image-based characterization
  • Coordinated cross-laboratory collaboration (biology and physics teams)
  • Published results in Computational and Structural Biotechnology Journal (2023)

Membrane Proteome Analysis of Phytophthora parasitica

University Côte d'Azur · 2021–2024

Comprehensive proteomics study identifying key molecular components underlying sensing mechanisms in zoospores of Phytophthora parasitica, a critical pathogen in crop protection. This PhD research combined proteomic analysis with biophysical approaches to elucidate early plant-pathogen interactions.

Key Achievements

  • Identified key membrane proteome components of zoospore sensing
  • Published in Scientific Reports (2025)
  • Integrated proteomic and biophysical experimental approaches

Plant Protection Product Registration Dossiers

Biocontrol Technologies · 2025–Present

Preparation of comprehensive plant protection product registration dossiers under EU Regulation 1107/2009. This project involves compiling compliant scientific and regulatory data, managing IUCLID submissions, and coordinating with EFSA and EU Member State Competent Authorities.

Key Responsibilities

  • Compile scientific literature and database submissions for regulatory approval
  • Manage IUCLID systems and post-submission documentation
  • Liaise with regulatory authorities and manage timelines
  • Coordinate with external consultants and internal R&D teams

Microscopy & Image Analysis

INRAE-ISA Sophia Antipolis · InPhyNi · 2020–2024

Four years of hands-on microscopy and quantitative image analysis at INRAE-ISA Sophia Antipolis and the Institut de Physique de Nice (InPhyNi), France. Work spanned light, fluorescence, confocal, electron, and high-speed camera imaging across a range of microorganisms and plant tissues — from sample preparation through to custom MATLAB analysis pipelines.

Techniques

  • Light, fluorescence, confocal, and electron microscopy
  • High-speed video acquisition and motility analysis
  • Immunofluorescence labelling and live imaging sample preparation
  • Image analysis with ImageJ, Zeiss ZEN, Nikon, and custom MATLAB pipelines
