Interactive learning and student integration

BOOST Ambassador · University Côte d'Azur / INRAE, Nice, France · 2022

Activity overview

To support the integration and well-being of incoming students, I co-created and facilitated a campus Treasure Hunt as part of the MScBOOST Welcome Day on 19 September 2022, at the Institut Sophia Agrobiotech (ISA), Nice. The goal was to turn the first day of the Master's programme into something memorable: an opportunity to explore the research environment, meet supervisors and peers, and feel genuinely welcomed — before a single lecture had taken place.

Three mixed groups of students (Ladybugs, Beetles, Spiders) each followed a unique route across 5 locations on the ISA campus. At every stop, they had to answer a question — scientific or community-oriented — to unlock the clue leading to the next location. BOOST ambassadors accompanied each group, creating an informal space for students to ask questions, share concerns, and connect with people who had recently navigated the same transition.

These activities were grounded in a broader vision of teaching as a human as well as scientific endeavour: building empathy, fostering teamwork, and establishing a sense of belonging from the very first day. Anonymous feedback was collected afterwards to continuously improve the experience.

Format

  • 3 groups of 4–5 students, each with a dedicated BOOST ambassador guide
  • 5 campus locations per route, each with a question-and-clue challenge
  • Mix of scientific, community, and orientation-based questions
  • Informal group discussions with ambassadors at each stop
  • Followed by a programme introduction and collective debrief session
  • Anonymous written feedback collected from all participants
